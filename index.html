The Indian Gauraiya (House Sparrow)

(Passer domesticus indicus)

Gauraiya is a small, social bird commonly found in urban and rural areas across India. It has a stout body, brownish-grey plumage, and a short, conical beak suited for seed-eating. Known for its close association with human settlements, the Gauraiya builds nests in homes, buildings, and crevices. Once abundant, its population has declined due to habitat loss, pollution, and changing urban landscapes. Despite this, conservation efforts aim to protect this familiar and cherished bird, which holds cultural significance in Indian folklore and daily life.
